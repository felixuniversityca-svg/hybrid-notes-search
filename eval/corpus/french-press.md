# Brewing with a french press

A french press is a tall cylinder with a mesh plunger. You add coarse grounds,
pour hot water just off the boil, let it steep for about four minutes, then press
the plunger down slowly to separate the grounds from the liquid. Because the mesh
lets oils through, the cup is fuller and heavier than filtered coffee. A coarse
grind matters: grind too fine and the press clogs and the brew turns bitter.
