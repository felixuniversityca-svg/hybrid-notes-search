# Mitochondria

Mitochondria are the organelles that supply most of a cell's usable energy. They
take in nutrients and oxygen and, through a chain of reactions, convert them into
ATP, the molecule cells spend to do work. They carry their own small loop of DNA,
separate from the genome in the nucleus, which is inherited only from the mother.
Cells that do a lot of work, like muscle and nerve cells, are packed with far
more of them than cells that sit idle.
