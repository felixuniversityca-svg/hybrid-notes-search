# Compound interest

When savings earn interest, that interest is added to the balance and then earns
interest of its own. Each period the base grows, so the gains accelerate rather
than staying flat. Left untouched for many years, a modest deposit can grow into
a much larger sum, and the longer the horizon the more dramatic the effect. This
snowballing is why starting early matters more than the exact rate, and why
withdrawing along the way quietly removes the part of the balance that would have
done the most compounding later.
